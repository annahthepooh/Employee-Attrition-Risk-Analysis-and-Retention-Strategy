# HR-Attrition-Diagnostic
A comprehensive analysis of attrition using Excel and Tableau
Project: Why is Attrition Happening? (HR Diagnostic)
## Executive Summary
This project analyzes attrition for a workforce of 1,470 employees. By testing three key hypotheses regarding salary, workload, and environment, I identified specific insights and provided actionable recommendations to improve retention from 83.9% to a higher target.
________________________________________
## The Workflow (The Documentation)
### 1. Data Cleaning & Preparation (Excel)

•	Renamed and reordered the columns using **power query**.<br/>
•	Handled missing values and verified data types for 30+ features.<br/>
•	Checked for more transformation in each column.

### 2. Hypothesis Testing

•	Hypothesis 1 **(Salary)**: Do people leave because they are underpaid? [Confirmed]<br/>
•	Hypothesis 2 **(Workload)**: Does overtime lead to higher attrition? [Confirmed]<br/>
•	Hypothesis 3 **(Environment)**: Does low satisfaction correlate with quitting? [Confirmed]

________________________________________
### Key Insights (The Findings)
•	The Salary Floor: Those who left the company earned an average of **$2046 less per month** than those who stayed.<br/>
•	The Overtime Impact: Employees working overtime have an attrition rate of **30.5%**, compared to only **10.4%** for those who don’t.<br/>
•	The Satisfaction Peak: Attrition hits its highest point **(20.6%)** among employees who rate their relationship satisfaction as Low (1/4).

### Final Recommendations
1.	Compensation Adjustment: Target entry-level salary increases to close the $1,400 gap.
2.	Overtime Audit: Review the Research & Development and Sales department to reduce burnout.
3.	Environment Check: Add a suggestion box to get insights from employees on how to improve the work environment.
